2. Virtual Conduct Rules

Together matching rooms connect real individuals globally for real-time study and work. To maintain a safe, welcoming, and productive environment for neurodivergent individuals and students, you agree to strictly adhere to the following conduct rules:

  • Work & Study Purpose: Focus rooms exist solely for work, study, chores, and quiet task management. You must not use the matches for socializing, dating, marketing, or general non-focus purposes.
  • Decency & Attire: If camera connection is active, you must remain fully clothed, display an appropriate, respectful environment, and avoid displaying any offensive, harassing, or graphic material.
  • Respectful Interaction: In voice mode, verbal communication must remain polite and encouraging. Harassment, hate speech, bullying, swearing, or disruptive behavior is met with a zero-tolerance policy.
  • Reporting System: Users can flag partners who violate these rules. If a camera-session report is submitted, Together attempts to capture and send a short proof clip and both users’ report/session data for human review.
  • Enforcement: Confirmed first-time abuse may result in a 30-day restriction from camera and partner services. Repeat abuse may result in a permanent restriction.

3. Camera Reports & Proof Clips

Before entering a camera body-doubling session, you must read and agree to the camera safety notice in the app. If one user reports another during a camera session, Together automatically attempts to capture a short 2-second proof video from the active session and upload it to private Together moderation storage for human review.

The report sent for review includes both users’ account IDs, display names, the session task, planned duration, elapsed time, connection style, session type, report reason, moderation status, timestamps, and the proof clip when capture and upload are available. Report records and proof clips are used to review abuse reports, enforce restrictions, protect users, and keep partner sessions safe.
