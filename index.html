The Science of
ADHD Body Doubling

Body doubling is an empirically backed focus strategy popular in the neurodivergent community. By working on tasks in the physical or virtual presence of another individual, your brain creates a passive field of social accountability.

For ADHD brains facing executive dysfunction, starting a task represents a massive cognitive hurdle. Having a "body double" working alongside you stabilizes dopamine levels, reduces the feeling of task-related dread, and gently prompts you back to focus if your attention wanders.

Together takes this principle and digitizes it. We offer peer-matching with real humans starting similar tasks, or a structured virtual study companion (Liam) who acts as a non-judgmental accountability anchor.
