Together vs Focusmate: Feature Comparison & Differences

A comprehensive, neutral breakdown of Focusmate and Together to help individuals with ADHD and neurodivergent focus styles choose the optimal virtual body doubling platform.

AT
Written by Ava Thalheim • Updated July 2026
lightbulb Key Takeaways (Comparison Breakdown)

Together vs Focusmate Summary:

  • Focusmate Strengths: Established web platform with a global user base, 25, 50, and 75-minute calendar-scheduled sessions, and structured video introductions.
  • Together Strengths: Designed for ADHD task initiation. Offers 3 session modes (Solo, 1:1 Live Peer, and 24/7 prerecorded Focus Spaces), Physical vs Mental task category matching, Companion Spaces (focusing alongside Kovu the dog), and native iOS/Android screenshot protection.
  • Core Differences: Focusmate defaults to desktop web calendar pre-booking with mandatory video. Together provides on-demand mobile and web matching with zero pre-booking friction and flexible Silent/Voice/Camera connection modes.

Deep Feature & Workflow Breakdown

1. Session Booking & Task Activation Friction

For individuals experiencing ADHD executive dysfunction, committing to a future time block on a calendar can induce task dread or scheduling friction. Focusmate operates primarily on scheduled calendar bookings. You must reserve a slot, show up on time, and match with someone booked for that exact duration.

Together removes pre-scheduling barriers by offering on-demand matching. When you feel ready to start, you create a custom task, pick a category, and get matched immediately with a peer online.

2. Task Category Matching: Physical vs. Mental

Body doubling for desk work (writing code, reading papers) requires a quiet, low-movement visual presence. Conversely, body doubling for physical chores (cleaning the kitchen, folding laundry, packing) involves moving around the room.

Together explicitly separates task creation into two core categories:

  • Physical Tasks: Cleaning, tidying, movement, packing, household chores.
  • Mental Tasks: Writing, coding, studying, reading, administrative tasks.

This ensures you are paired with a partner engaged in a similar movement spectrum, maintaining visual alignment and preventing distraction.

3. Connection Modes & Social Battery Protection

Mandatory webcam video can cause camera fatigue or anxiety for neurodivergent users on low-energy days. While Focusmate requires camera presence during sessions, Together allows users to choose between Silent Mode (presence-only progress indicators), Voice Mode (verbal check-ins without video), and Camera Mode.

4. Focus Spaces & Companion Spaces

When you want focus companionship without any human interaction, Together provides 24/7 prerecorded Focus Spaces. This includes special Companion Spaces where you can focus alongside Kovu, the founder's dog, providing a soothing, pressure-free focus environment.
